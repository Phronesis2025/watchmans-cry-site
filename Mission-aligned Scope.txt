6. Moral and Religious Foundations: Role of faith in society, providence in nation-building, family as societal bedrock, and ethical leadership (e.g., Franklin's proverbs on virtue; many invoked biblical principles).

7. Justice, Law, and Equality: Rule of law, fair trials, opposition to slavery/arbitrary power (evolving views), and pursuit of a "more perfect union" (e.g., debates in Constitutional Convention).

8. Innovation, Self-Reliance, and Progress: Practical wisdom, invention, agriculture/manufacturing independence, and iterative improvement (e.g., Franklin's experiments; Jefferson's agrarian ideals).
